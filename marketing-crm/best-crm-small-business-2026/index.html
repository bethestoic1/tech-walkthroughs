Best CRM for Small Business 2026

We stress-tested leading CRMs for integration maturity, AI features, and value. See our methodology.

Choosing a CRM for a small business is overwhelming: every vendor promises the moon. We ran HubSpot, Monday.com, and two other finalists through real workflows—contacts, pipelines, automation, and AI features—to see which actually delivers. Here’s what we found.

How we evaluated

  • Integration maturity — Native integrations, API, and play-nice with your existing stack.
  • AI features — Does the AI save time (drafts, lead scoring, next steps) or just look good?
  • Pricing — Clear tiers, no surprise limits, scalable as you grow.
  • Ease of use — Can a small team get value without a full-time admin?

Top picks

HubSpot — Our market leader. AI-first ops, strong integrations, and a free tier that’s genuinely useful. Best for teams that want one platform for marketing, sales, and service. Downsides: cost scales up; some features are paywalled.

Monday.com — Best for value and flexibility. Works as CRM and project/ops hub. Customizable; good for teams that don’t want to be locked into a single workflow. Slightly steeper learning curve for “pure” sales CRM use.

Others we tested — We also evaluated alternatives focused on simplicity or niche use; they’re in the table below where relevant.

Tool Integration AI Price (entry) Best for

Verdict

Our pick: HubSpot — if you want the most mature AI and integrations and can invest in the platform. Best value: Monday.com — if you need a flexible, cost-effective CRM that doubles as ops hub.
