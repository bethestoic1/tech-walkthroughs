Best CRM for Real Estate 2026

We evaluated CRMs for real estate agents and brokerages on pipeline management, lead automation, and long-term contact nurturing. See our methodology.

Real estate CRM needs are specific: long sales cycles, high lead volumes, follow-up cadences that span months, and the need to track relationships across buyers, sellers, and referral partners simultaneously. Generic CRMs can handle this, but the best ones make it frictionless. We evaluated the top options in 2026 - from general-purpose CRMs adapted for real estate to platforms purpose-built for agents and brokerages.

How we evaluated

  • Pipeline management - Visual deal stages for buyer and seller pipelines, ease of moving contacts through stages, and pipeline reporting.
  • Lead capture & routing - Web forms, landing pages, lead source tracking, and automatic routing to the right agent.
  • Automated follow-up - Email sequences, drip campaigns, and activity reminders - the core of real estate nurture workflows.
  • Contact & relationship management - Tagging, segmentation, notes, activity history, and long-term contact nurturing across multi-year cycles.
  • Integrations - MLS feeds, Zillow/Realtor.com lead sync, email, calendar, and marketing tools.
  • Price vs. value - What you get per seat/month at each tier, with attention to features that matter for real estate specifically.

Monday CRM - best for visual pipelines and flexibility

Monday CRM is our top pick for most real estate agents and small-to-mid brokerages in 2026. It's a general-purpose CRM with enough real estate configurability - custom pipeline stages, contact segmentation, activity tracking, and automated reminders - to handle buyer, seller, and referral workflows without the per-seat premium of purpose-built tools.

Pros

  • Visual pipeline boards - Drag-and-drop deal stages that you configure to match your actual buyer/seller workflow. Easy to see deal status at a glance across your entire book.
  • Flexible contact management - Custom fields for property preferences, budget, timeline, referral source, and anything else relevant. Works for buyers, sellers, investors, and referral partners in separate boards.
  • Automated reminders and follow-up sequences - Set automations to remind you to follow up after X days of no activity, send emails on trigger events, or move deals to a new stage automatically.
  • Fast onboarding - Real estate templates available out of the box. A solo agent can be running a functional pipeline in under a day.
  • Integrations with email and calendar - Gmail, Outlook, Google Calendar, and Zapier (for Zillow/Realtor.com lead sync if native integration is needed).

Cons

  • No native MLS integration - You'll need Zapier or manual entry to sync MLS leads. Purpose-built tools like Follow Up Boss handle this natively.
  • Not built for real estate specifically - Reporting, terminology, and defaults are generic. You'll configure your way to a real estate CRM rather than starting with one.
  • Per-seat cost - At $12–$19/seat/month (Standard to Pro), a team of 10 agents costs $1,200–$1,900/month. Follow Up Boss may be more cost-effective for large teams.

Pricing

  • Basic CRM - $12/seat/month. Pipelines, contact management, activity log.
  • Standard CRM - $17/seat/month. Automations, email tracking, advanced pipelines.
  • Pro CRM - $28/seat/month. Sales forecasting, custom reports, quota tracking.
  • Enterprise - Custom pricing. SSO, advanced security, account management.

HubSpot CRM - best free starting point for brokerages

HubSpot CRM's free tier is genuinely the best zero-cost CRM option for real estate in 2026. Unlimited contacts, basic pipeline management, email tracking, and deal stages - all free. For brokerages that need marketing automation (drip campaigns, landing pages, lead capture forms), HubSpot's paid tiers are competitive. The trade-off is price at scale: HubSpot's Marketing Hub can get expensive for high-volume lead nurturing operations.

Pros

  • Free forever - unlimited contacts - No contact limits on the free CRM. A solo agent or small team can manage their entire database without paying anything.
  • Best-in-class email marketing - HubSpot's email sequences, drip campaigns, and marketing automation are among the strongest in the industry. Key for real estate's long nurture cycles.
  • Lead capture and landing pages - Built-in form builder and landing pages for capturing buyer/seller leads from your website. Eliminates the need for a separate lead gen tool.
  • AI-powered features - HubSpot AI can write email sequences, summarize contact activity, and suggest next actions. More developed than most competitors.
  • Reporting and dashboards - Detailed pipeline reporting, deal velocity, and lead source attribution. Useful for brokerages tracking agent performance.

Cons

  • Marketing automation is expensive at scale - HubSpot Marketing Hub Pro starts at $800/month for 2,000 contacts. For high-volume lead operations, costs scale quickly.
  • No native MLS integration - Like Monday CRM, MLS sync requires Zapier or third-party connectors.
  • Complexity at higher tiers - HubSpot's full suite is powerful but can overwhelm solo agents who just need a simple pipeline and follow-up system.

Pricing

  • Free CRM - Unlimited contacts, 1 pipeline, basic email tracking, deal stages.
  • Sales Hub Starter - From $15/seat/month. Sequences, meeting scheduling, email templates.
  • Sales Hub Pro - From $90/seat/month. Forecasting, custom reporting, playbooks, call coaching.
  • Marketing Hub Pro - From $800/month (contact-based). Full automation, landing pages, A/B testing.

Follow Up Boss - best purpose-built real estate CRM

Follow Up Boss is built specifically for real estate - it's the only CRM on this list that has native integrations with Zillow, Realtor.com, Facebook Leads, and most MLS portals. The interface is designed around real estate workflows: speed-to-lead automation, automatic lead routing to agents, and drip campaigns built for buyer and seller nurture. The downside is price - at $69/user/month it's significantly more expensive than general-purpose CRMs.

Pros

  • Native MLS and portal integrations - Direct integrations with Zillow, Realtor.com, BoomTown, Ylopo, and more. Leads from portals flow in automatically with source tracking.
  • Speed-to-lead automation - Automatically assigns and routes new leads to agents in seconds, with built-in follow-up sequences that start immediately. Critical for portal lead conversion.
  • Built for real estate terminology - Buyer and seller pipelines, transaction coordination stages, and reporting built around real estate KPIs (not adapted from a generic sales tool).
  • Team management for brokerages - Agent performance dashboards, lead distribution rules, and brokerage-level reporting.

Cons

  • Expensive - $69/user/month for the standard plan. A 5-agent team costs $345/month; a 20-agent brokerage pays $1,380/month. Monday CRM covers similar ground for half the price.
  • Less flexible for non-standard workflows - Purpose-built means opinionated. If your brokerage has unusual workflows or you need to manage non-real-estate relationships, Follow Up Boss is limiting.
  • No free tier - 14-day trial only. You need to commit before you fully evaluate it.

Pricing

  • Grow - $69/user/month. Full CRM, all integrations, email/SMS sequences.
  • Pro - $99/user/month. Dedicated account manager, advanced reporting.
  • Platform - Custom pricing. API access, white-label options, enterprise support.

FAQ

Do I need a real-estate-specific CRM?

Not necessarily. If your main source of leads is your own network and referrals (not Zillow/Realtor.com portals), a general-purpose CRM like Monday CRM or HubSpot handles the workflow just as well - at a lower price. If you're running a high-volume portal lead operation or a multi-agent brokerage that needs speed-to-lead automation and native MLS sync, Follow Up Boss earns its higher price.

Is HubSpot CRM really free for real estate?

The core CRM is free with no contact limits - genuinely useful for a solo agent or small team to manage relationships and track deals. The expensive parts are HubSpot's Marketing Hub (for email automation at scale) and Sales Hub Pro (for sequences and forecasting). Many real estate teams use HubSpot's free CRM with a separate email marketing tool to keep costs down.

How many agents does a brokerage need before Follow Up Boss is worth it?

The ROI equation depends heavily on lead source. If you're running a high-volume Zillow Premier Agent operation and speed-to-lead conversion is critical, Follow Up Boss can pay for itself quickly. For 5 or fewer agents primarily working referrals and their own lead gen, Monday CRM or HubSpot is almost certainly the better value.

Verdict

Best for most agents and small teams: Monday CRM - visual pipelines, fast setup, flexible enough to handle any real estate workflow, at a lower per-seat cost than purpose-built alternatives. Start here unless you have a specific need for MLS integrations.

Best free starting point: HubSpot CRM - unlimited contacts at no cost, with best-in-class email marketing when you're ready to invest. Ideal for brokerages that want to grow into a full marketing automation stack.

Best for portal-heavy brokerages: Follow Up Boss - the only CRM with native Zillow/Realtor.com integration and real-estate-specific speed-to-lead automation. Worth the premium if portals are your primary lead source.
