Best VPN for Business 2026

We tested business VPNs for speed, no-logs policies, team features, and compliance. See our methodology.

Remote work isn’t going away. Whether your team is fully distributed, hybrid, or just has a few road warriors, a business VPN protects company data on public Wi-Fi, enforces access policies, and keeps your traffic private. We tested NordVPN, ExpressVPN, Surfshark, and Perimeter 81 across real business scenarios—speed, admin controls, compliance, and cost—to find the best options for teams of 5–200 in 2026.

How we evaluated

  • Speed & reliability — Download/upload throughput across US, EU, and APAC servers. Consistency matters more than peak speed for day-to-day work (video calls, cloud apps, file transfers).
  • No-logs policy — Independent audit results, jurisdiction (Panama, BVI, Netherlands), and transparency reports. For business use, "we don’t log" isn’t enough—we want receipts.
  • Team management — Central admin dashboard, user provisioning, billing per seat, and SSO/SCIM support for larger teams.
  • Compliance & security — Kill switch reliability, AES-256 encryption, dedicated IPs, and fit for SOC 2 / GDPR / HIPAA environments.
  • Pricing & value — Per-seat cost, contract flexibility (monthly vs. annual vs. 2-year), and what you actually get at each tier.

Top picks at a glance

NordVPN — Our market leader. The best balance of speed, independently audited no-logs, and business-grade features. NordLayer (their business product) adds team management, SSO, and network segmentation on top of NordVPN’s consumer infrastructure.

ExpressVPN — Premium alternative. Historically the fastest consumer VPN with a strong trust reputation. Business offering is newer but solid. Best if you prioritize simplicity and are willing to pay more per seat.

NordVPN / NordLayer: detailed breakdown

Nord Security offers two products: NordVPN (consumer/individual) and NordLayer (business). NordLayer is the one you want for teams—it wraps NordVPN’s infrastructure with a central admin panel, team gateways, and network access controls. Both share the same 6,400+ server network across 111 countries.

Pros

  • Independently audited no-logs — Four audits by Deloitte and PricewaterhouseCoopers since 2018. Panama jurisdiction (no data retention laws). This is the gold standard for VPN trust in 2026.
  • Fast and consistent — NordLynx protocol (based on WireGuard) delivered 850+ Mbps in our US tests, 600+ Mbps to EU servers. Video calls and large file transfers were smooth across all regions.
  • NordLayer admin panel — Add/remove users, assign teams to specific gateways, enforce always-on VPN, and monitor usage. SSO via Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace, and OneLogin.
  • Dedicated IP and servers — Fixed IPs for allowlisting on firewalls, cloud dashboards, and SaaS tools that restrict access by IP.
  • Threat Protection — Built-in malware blocking, ad blocking, and phishing protection at the DNS level. One less tool to manage.
  • Split tunneling — Route only business traffic through the VPN; let personal browsing and streaming go direct. Reduces bandwidth load and improves speed for non-sensitive apps.

Cons

  • NordLayer is a separate product — If you already have NordVPN personal accounts, you can’t just "upgrade" them. NordLayer requires a separate subscription and admin setup.
  • No phone support on basic tiers — Priority support and a dedicated account manager require the higher-tier plans.
  • SCIM provisioning is Enterprise-only — Auto-provisioning from your identity provider requires the top tier ($9+/user/month).
  • Overkill for solo freelancers — If you’re a team of one, consumer NordVPN ($3–5/mo) is sufficient. NordLayer’s value kicks in at 5+ users.

Pricing (NordLayer for teams)

  • Lite — $8/user/month (annual). Shared gateways, SSO, basic admin panel, unlimited devices.
  • Core — $11/user/month. Dedicated gateways, always-on VPN, device posture checks, activity monitoring.
  • Premium — $14/user/month. Network segmentation, browser-based access, custom DNS.
  • Enterprise — Custom pricing. SCIM, dedicated account manager, priority support, SLA guarantees.

Consumer NordVPN (for individuals or very small teams): $3.59/mo on 2-year plan, up to 10 devices.

ExpressVPN: detailed breakdown

ExpressVPN has long been the "premium" consumer VPN, known for speed and polished apps. Their business product (ExpressVPN for Teams) adds centralized billing and user management while keeping the same server network and Lightway protocol.

Pros

  • Excellent speed — Lightway protocol consistently hits 800+ Mbps. One of the fastest VPNs we tested, especially on mobile devices.
  • Simple setup — Apps for every platform (Windows, Mac, Linux, iOS, Android, routers). Install and connect in under 2 minutes. Minimal configuration needed.
  • Strong trust signals — No-logs policy verified by KPMG and Cure53. RAM-only servers (TrustedServer) that wipe on every reboot. BVI jurisdiction.
  • 94 country coverage — Servers in 94+ countries. Good for teams with employees in regions where coverage matters (Southeast Asia, Middle East, Africa).

Cons

  • Tiered pricing adds complexity — ExpressVPN restructured to three tiers in late 2025 (Basic $3.49/mo, Advanced $4.49/mo, Pro $7.49/mo on 2-year plans). The entry price is now competitive, but full features require the Pro tier, which still runs higher than NordLayer per seat when managing a team.
  • Business features less mature — Admin dashboard is functional but not as deep as NordLayer. No network segmentation or device posture checks yet.
  • Device limits vary by tier — Basic allows 10 simultaneous devices (up from 8 in 2025); NordVPN allows 10; NordLayer has unlimited. For teams with many devices per person, compare tier limits.
  • No built-in threat protection — No DNS-level malware or phishing blocking included. You’ll need a separate endpoint security tool.

Pricing

  • Basic (2-year) — $3.49/mo. Core VPN, 10 devices.
  • Advanced (2-year) — $4.49/mo. Adds threat manager, ad blocker, 12 devices.
  • Pro (2-year) — $7.49/mo. Adds identity protection features, 14 devices.
  • Teams — Custom pricing (contact sales). Central billing, user management, dedicated support.

Others we tested

Surfshark

Unlimited devices per account at $2.19/mo (2-year plan)—the best raw value. CleanWeb blocks ads and malware. No-logs audited by Deloitte. The business product (Surfshark for Teams) is early-stage; admin features lag behind NordLayer. Good for budget-conscious small teams that don’t need advanced network controls.

Perimeter 81 (now part of Check Point)

Purpose-built for business from day one: zero-trust network access, cloud firewall, and granular access policies. Starts at $8/user/month. Best for mid-market teams (50+) that need ZTNA rather than a traditional VPN. Overkill and pricier for teams under 20 who just need encrypted tunnels.

When to choose what

  • Choose NordVPN / NordLayer if you want the best all-around business VPN: audited trust, fast speeds, solid admin tools, and competitive per-seat pricing.
  • Choose ExpressVPN if simplicity is paramount and you’re willing to pay a premium. Great for teams that want "install and forget" without complex network configuration.
  • Choose Surfshark if budget is tight and you need unlimited devices per seat. Accept that business admin features are still maturing.
  • Choose Perimeter 81 if you need zero-trust network access, cloud firewalls, and granular policy controls—typically for regulated or larger teams.

When to avoid a business VPN

  • You only need site-to-site connectivity — Consider a cloud-native solution like Tailscale or WireGuard on your own infrastructure instead.
  • Your team is 1–2 people on trusted networks — A consumer VPN plan is probably sufficient. Save the per-seat cost for when you scale.
  • You need full zero-trust architecture — A VPN alone isn’t ZTNA. Look at Perimeter 81, Zscaler, or Cloudflare Access if "trust nothing, verify everything" is the requirement.

FAQ

Do I need a "business VPN" or can I just buy consumer VPN licenses?

For teams under 5, consumer plans (NordVPN, ExpressVPN) work fine—each person manages their own account. Once you hit 5+ users, a business product saves time: central billing, user provisioning, and the ability to enforce always-on connections and revoke access when someone leaves.

Will a VPN slow down my team’s internet?

Modern protocols (NordLynx, Lightway, WireGuard) add 5–15% overhead at most on fast connections. For typical business tasks—email, cloud apps, video calls—the difference is unnoticeable. Use split tunneling to route only sensitive traffic through the VPN if speed is critical.

Is a VPN enough for compliance (HIPAA, SOC 2, GDPR)?

A VPN is one layer, not the whole stack. It encrypts data in transit, but compliance also requires endpoint security, access controls, audit logging, and data handling policies. A business VPN with a no-logs audit (like NordLayer) is a strong foundation—pair it with a password manager (see our picks) and endpoint protection for full coverage.

What about WireGuard vs. built-in VPN?

Self-hosted WireGuard is faster and cheaper at scale, but you manage your own servers, updates, and configuration. Business VPNs like NordLayer handle all of that. For most small teams, managed beats self-hosted on time savings alone.
