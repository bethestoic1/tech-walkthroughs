NordVPN vs Surfshark 2026

We tested both VPNs for speed, security, no-logs practices, and business features. See our methodology.

NordVPN and Surfshark are the two most searched VPN comparisons in 2026—and for good reason. Both offer strong security, fast speeds, and competitive pricing. But they make different trade-offs: NordVPN leads on speed, server network, and enterprise features; Surfshark wins on price, unlimited devices, and value for personal or small-team use. We ran both through real-world tests to give you a clear answer on which is right for your situation.

How we evaluated

  • Speed — Download and upload speeds on local, regional, and international servers. Measured at consistent times using multiple test runs.
  • Security & privacy — Encryption standard, no-logs policy, jurisdiction, audit history, and leak protection.
  • Server network — Number of servers, countries covered, and specialty server availability (obfuscated, double VPN, P2P).
  • Business & team features — Dedicated IP, Teams/Business plans, admin controls, and compliance posture.
  • Price & value — Monthly cost, annual pricing, number of simultaneous connections, and what you get at each tier.

Quick verdict

NordVPN wins on speed, server count, and business-grade features. It's the better choice for teams, remote employees, and anyone who needs consistent performance across many locations.

Surfshark wins on price and simultaneous connections. Unlimited devices on one plan makes it the best value for households, freelancers, and small teams who don't need enterprise features.

NordVPN: detailed breakdown

NordVPN is the market leader with over 6,300 servers in 111 countries. In 2026 it remains the benchmark for speed, security audits, and business functionality. NordVPN Teams and Dedicated IP options make it viable for organizations that need consistent exit IPs and centralized billing.

Pros

  • Fastest speeds in class — NordVPN's NordLynx protocol (WireGuard-based) consistently leads independent speed benchmarks. Real-world download speed reductions average under 10% on nearby servers.
  • 6,300+ servers in 111 countries — The largest server network of any mainstream VPN. More servers = less congestion = better speeds at peak times.
  • Double VPN and obfuscated servers — Routes traffic through two servers for extra anonymity, or obfuscates traffic to bypass VPN detection in restrictive networks.
  • Audited no-logs policy — Multiple third-party audits (PricewaterhouseCoopers, Deloitte) confirm no user activity or connection logs are stored.
  • Dedicated IP + Teams plan — Fixed IP address for consistent access to business resources; Teams dashboard for centralized billing and account management.
  • Threat Protection — Built-in malware scanning, ad blocking, and tracker protection. No separate tool needed.

Cons

  • Pricier than Surfshark — Standard plan starts around $3.99/month on a 2-year plan. More expensive than Surfshark for comparable coverage.
  • 6 simultaneous connections on standard plans — Surfshark offers unlimited; NordVPN caps at 6 (10 on Ultimate plan) unless you're on a Teams account.
  • No free tier — 30-day money-back guarantee but no permanent free plan. Surfshark and others offer limited free options.

Pricing

  • Basic — From ~$3.99/month (2-year plan). VPN + malware protection, 6 devices.
  • Plus — From ~$4.99/month. Adds password manager and data breach scanner.
  • Ultimate — From ~$6.99/month. Adds cyber insurance, 10 devices.
  • Teams — From ~$7/user/month. Centralized billing, dedicated account manager, team dashboard.

Surfshark: detailed breakdown

Surfshark launched in 2018 and rapidly positioned itself as the best-value premium VPN. Its key differentiator is unlimited simultaneous connections — one plan covers every device you own plus your whole family or team. In 2026 it has expanded to 3,200+ servers in 100 countries and added CleanWeb (ad and tracker blocking) and Nexus (a network of connected VPN nodes for more stable routing).

Pros

  • Unlimited simultaneous connections — One subscription covers every device with no cap. Ideal for households, freelancers, or small teams sharing a plan.
  • Best-in-class value — Starter plan from ~$2.19/month on a 2-year deal. Significantly cheaper than NordVPN for comparable single-user protection.
  • CleanWeb — Built-in ad blocker, malware blocker, and phishing protection. Comparable to NordVPN's Threat Protection.
  • Nexus technology — Routes traffic through a network of nodes rather than a single server, improving stability and making it harder to detect as VPN traffic.
  • No-logs policy (audited) — Deloitte audit confirms no activity or connection logs stored. Based in the Netherlands (EU jurisdiction).
  • Alert feature — Monitors for data breaches, leaked credentials, and credit card exposure. Included on higher plans.

Cons

  • Smaller server network — 3,200 servers vs NordVPN's 6,300. Fewer options = more congestion in popular locations at peak times.
  • Slightly slower on long-distance connections — NordVPN's NordLynx edges ahead on cross-continental speeds in independent tests. For most users the difference is imperceptible; for high-demand streaming or transfers it can matter.
  • No dedicated IP on lower plans — Dedicated IP requires the Business/Teams plan. NordVPN offers it as an add-on on personal plans.
  • Less mature business offering — Surfshark for Teams exists but has fewer enterprise controls than NordVPN Teams.

Pricing

  • Starter — From ~$2.19/month (2-year plan). VPN + ad/malware blocking, unlimited devices.
  • One — From ~$2.69/month. Adds antivirus and data breach alerts.
  • One+ — From ~$4.29/month. Adds data removal service from broker databases.

When to choose NordVPN

  • Your team needs a dedicated IP for consistent access to company resources or whitelisted IPs.
  • You need the fastest possible speeds — video calls, large file transfers, or streaming 4K internationally.
  • You want the largest server selection for a specific country or region.
  • You need a mature Teams dashboard with centralized billing and per-user management.

When to choose Surfshark

  • You want to cover unlimited devices on one plan — whole household, all your devices, or a small team sharing a subscription.
  • Budget is the primary driver and you don't need enterprise-grade controls.
  • You want antivirus + VPN bundled at a low price (Surfshark One).
  • You're an individual or freelancer who doesn't need dedicated IP or team management.

FAQ

Is NordVPN worth more than Surfshark?

For most individual users, no — Surfshark's speed and security are close enough that the price premium doesn't pay off. Where NordVPN earns its higher price: teams needing dedicated IPs, users who need the absolute fastest speeds on international connections, and organizations that need mature admin controls. For personal use, Surfshark delivers 95% of the value at 55% of the price.

Can I use Surfshark for business?

Yes, Surfshark for Teams exists and works well for small businesses. It supports centralized billing, team member management, and static IPs. For larger organizations or those requiring dedicated account management and more enterprise-grade controls, NordVPN Teams is the stronger option.

Do both VPNs work in China and restrictive networks?

Both offer obfuscated servers designed to bypass VPN detection — NordVPN's obfuscated servers and Surfshark's NoBorders mode. Neither guarantees functionality in China (the Great Firewall evolves constantly), but both have a better track record than most competitors. Check their current status before traveling.

Verdict

Our pick for teams and speed: NordVPN — the fastest speeds, largest server network, audited no-logs policy, and the most mature Teams offering. Worth the premium for businesses and power users.

Best value for individuals and unlimited devices: Surfshark — covers every device you own for under $2.50/month on a 2-year plan. The best dollar-for-dollar VPN if you don't need enterprise features.
