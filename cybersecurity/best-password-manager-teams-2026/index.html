Best Password Manager for Teams 2026

We tested team password managers for security, sharing, and admin controls. See our methodology.

Shared passwords are a security disaster waiting to happen. Sticky notes, shared spreadsheets, "just Slack me the login" - every team has been there. A business password manager gives your team shared vaults, role-based access, breach monitoring, and secure onboarding/offboarding. We tested NordPass Business, 1Password, Bitwarden, Dashlane, and Keeper across security, sharing, admin controls, and cost to find the best options for teams of 5-200 in 2026.

How we evaluated

  • Security model - Zero-knowledge architecture, encryption standard (AES-256), Secret Key / master password design, and independent audit history.
  • Breach monitoring - Does it alert you when team credentials appear in known breaches? How fast and how actionable are the alerts?
  • Sharing & vaults - Shared collections, granular permissions (read-only vs. edit), guest access for contractors, and vault organization.
  • Admin & compliance - SSO (SAML/OIDC), SCIM provisioning, audit logs, custom security policies, and compliance certifications (SOC 2, GDPR).
  • Ease of rollout - How fast can you deploy to 20 people? Browser extension quality, mobile apps, and onboarding friction.
  • Value - Per-seat pricing, what's included at each tier, and hidden costs.

Top picks at a glance

NordPass Business - Best value for teams. XChaCha20 encryption, shared vaults with role control, dark web monitoring, and admin console at $4.99/user/month. Ideal for teams already in the Nord ecosystem (NordVPN, NordLayer).

1Password - Best UX and security architecture. Watchtower breach monitoring, Secret Key dual-factor encryption, excellent vault sharing, and strong admin controls. The most polished experience for teams.

Bitwarden - Best for budget and open source. Fully audited, self-hosting option, and the most affordable per-seat pricing. More transparent and flexible than the competition.

NordPass Business: detailed breakdown

NordPass Business is Nord Security's team password manager - same XChaCha20 encryption as the consumer plan, plus an admin console, shared vaults, activity logs, and dark web monitoring. At $4.99/user/month it undercuts 1Password Business while covering all core team vault and admin needs. The standout advantage is ecosystem fit: teams already using NordVPN or NordLayer can consolidate under one Nord Security account with SSO via Azure AD and Google Workspace on Enterprise.

Pros

  • XChaCha20 encryption - A modern, fast cipher that's resistant to timing attacks. Forward-looking compared to the AES-256 standard used by most competitors.
  • Competitive per-seat pricing - $4.99/user/month (Business) is cheaper than 1Password Business ($7.99) and comparable to Bitwarden Teams ($4). Strong value for mid-size teams.
  • Shared vaults with role control - Assign view-only or edit access per vault. Instant offboarding revocation when team members leave.
  • Dark web monitoring - Scans for leaked credentials matching your company domain and alerts admins in real time.
  • Nord ecosystem synergy - Consolidate NordVPN, NordLayer, NordPass, and NordLocker under a single account. Fewer vendors to manage.
  • Clean, simple interface - Less onboarding friction than more complex tools. Non-technical team members adopt it quickly.

Cons

  • Newer product, less enterprise track record - NordPass Business is younger than 1Password Teams. Fewer case studies and integrations at the 500+ seat scale.
  • SSO requires Enterprise tier - SAML SSO via Azure AD and Google Workspace is only on the Enterprise plan ($7.99/user/month), same price tier as 1Password Business.
  • No self-hosting - Cloud-only. Teams with air-gapped or data-sovereignty requirements need Bitwarden.
  • Fewer admin integrations - Admin console is functional but lacks the depth of 1Password's reporting and custom policy controls.

Pricing

  • Teams - $4.99/user/month. Shared vaults, activity logs, admin panel, dark web monitoring.
  • Business - $7.99/user/month. SSO (Azure AD, Google Workspace), SCIM, advanced policies.
  • Enterprise - Custom pricing. Dedicated support, custom onboarding, SLA.

1Password: detailed breakdown

1Password has been the default recommendation for team password management for years. Their dual-key model (master password + Secret Key) means even 1Password can't access your data. In 2026, they've added passkey support, deeper SSO integration, and expanded Watchtower monitoring.

Pros

  • Best-in-class UX - The browser extension, desktop app, and mobile apps are the most polished in the category. Autofill is reliable across browsers. Team members actually use it (adoption is the hardest part of password management).
  • Watchtower - Monitors all stored credentials against known breaches, flags weak/reused passwords, and alerts on expiring 2FA. Actionable dashboard for admins to see team-wide password health at a glance.
  • Vault sharing done right - Create shared vaults per team, project, or client. Granular permissions (view-only, edit, manage). Guest accounts for contractors with automatic expiry.
  • SSO and SCIM - SAML SSO with Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace, OneLogin, and JumpCloud. SCIM auto-provisioning on Business tier. When someone leaves, disable their account and all shared access revokes instantly.
  • Secret Key architecture - Your master password alone isn't enough to decrypt. The Secret Key (stored on your devices, never sent to 1Password) adds a second factor at the encryption level. Even if 1Password's servers were breached, attackers can't decrypt vaults.
  • Passkey support - Store and autofill passkeys alongside passwords. As sites adopt passkeys, 1Password handles both in one place.

Cons

  • No self-hosting - Your encrypted vaults live on 1Password's cloud. For air-gapped or sovereign-data environments, this is a dealbreaker. Bitwarden is the alternative here.
  • Pricier than NordPass and Bitwarden - $7.99/user/month (Business) vs. NordPass Business at $4.99 and Bitwarden's $4/user/month. The UX premium is worth it for most teams, but budget-constrained organizations feel the difference at scale.
  • SCIM requires Business tier - Auto-provisioning from your identity provider needs the $7.99/user/month plan. Teams starter ($19.95/mo for up to 10 users) doesn't include it.
  • Secret Key adds onboarding friction - New team members need their Secret Key to set up new devices. The Emergency Kit PDF helps, but it's one more step vs. pure SSO-based onboarding.

Pricing

  • Teams Starter - $19.95/month for up to 10 users. Shared vaults, admin console, Watchtower, 2FA.
  • Business - $7.99/user/month. SSO, SCIM, advanced reporting, custom groups, VPN integration, 5 GB per user.
  • Enterprise - Custom pricing. Dedicated account manager, custom onboarding, tailored security policies, SLA.

Bitwarden: detailed breakdown

Bitwarden is the open-source alternative that's gained serious traction with security-conscious and budget-conscious teams. The entire codebase is public and audited. You can self-host on your own infrastructure or use their cloud. At $4/user/month for Teams, it's the cheapest option in the category.

Pros

  • Open source and audited - Full source code on GitHub. Audited by Cure53 and Insight Risk Consulting. You (or your security team) can verify exactly what the software does.
  • Self-hosting option - Run Bitwarden on your own servers using their official Docker images. Full control over where your data lives. The only major password manager that offers true self-hosting.
  • Most affordable - Teams at $4/user/month, Enterprise at $6/user/month. A 20-person team pays $80/mo vs. $100/mo on NordPass or $160/mo on 1Password Business.
  • Vault health reports - Breach monitoring (via Have I Been Pwned), weak password detection, reused password alerts, and exposed passwords. Similar to 1Password's Watchtower.
  • Wide platform support - Browser extensions for every major browser, desktop apps (Windows, Mac, Linux), mobile apps, and a CLI for automation.

Cons

  • UX is functional but less polished - The apps work well but feel more utilitarian than 1Password or NordPass. Autofill is reliable but occasionally requires an extra click. Team adoption may be slightly harder for non-technical users.
  • Self-hosting requires maintenance - You manage updates, backups, and uptime. Small teams without a sysadmin should use Bitwarden's cloud.
  • SSO is Enterprise-only - SAML SSO requires the $6/user/month Enterprise plan. 1Password includes SSO on its $7.99/user/month Business tier, but the price gap is still in Bitwarden's favor.
  • No Secret Key equivalent - Bitwarden uses a master password with PBKDF2/Argon2 hashing. Secure, but lacks the additional encryption layer of 1Password's Secret Key. A strong master password is essential.

Pricing

  • Teams - $4/user/month. Shared vaults, directory integration, event logs, priority support.
  • Enterprise - $6/user/month. SSO (SAML), SCIM provisioning, custom roles, enterprise policies, account recovery.
  • Self-hosted - Same pricing, or free if using the community (Vaultwarden) fork (unsupported).

Others we tested

Dashlane

Strong phishing detection and built-in VPN (unusual for a password manager). Business plan at $8/user/month. Polished UX, good autofill. Cons: pricier than both 1Password and Bitwarden, no self-hosting, and the built-in VPN is basic compared to a dedicated VPN. Best if you want password management + lightweight VPN in one subscription.

Keeper

Strong on compliance: FedRAMP authorized, ITAR compliant, SOC 2 and ISO 27001 certified. Business plan at $3.75/user/month (one of the cheapest). Robust admin controls and granular sharing policies. Cons: UX is dated compared to 1Password, and some features (advanced reporting, BreachWatch) cost extra. Best for government, defense, and highly regulated teams where compliance certifications matter most.

NordLocker

NordLocker is Nord Security's encrypted file vault and team cloud storage tool - a complement to NordPass rather than a replacement. Use it to protect sensitive documents (contracts, PII, credential stores) with client-side encryption before cloud sync. Teams on NordLocker Business get shared encrypted folders, admin access management, and audit logs. For teams already on NordVPN + NordPass, bundling NordLocker is cheaper than a standalone encrypted storage solution like Tresorit.

When to choose what

  • Choose NordPass Business if you want competitive per-seat pricing, a clean interface that drives quick team adoption, and you're already using NordVPN or NordLayer. Best value for teams under 100.
  • Choose 1Password if you want the best UX, easiest team adoption at scale, and strongest out-of-the-box security (Secret Key + Watchtower). Worth the premium for most teams.
  • Choose Bitwarden if budget matters, you want open-source transparency, or you need self-hosting for data sovereignty. Best value per seat.
  • Choose Dashlane if you want a bundled VPN and strong phishing detection without managing a separate VPN subscription.
  • Choose Keeper if compliance certifications (FedRAMP, ITAR, ISO 27001) are requirements, especially in government or defense.

When to avoid

  • Avoid NordPass if you need deep SCIM/SSO integrations at the Teams tier or have 500+ seat enterprise requirements that need proven large-scale deployments.
  • Avoid 1Password if you need self-hosting or air-gapped deployment. Their cloud-only model is a non-starter for some security environments.
  • Avoid Bitwarden if team adoption is your biggest risk and you need the most polished, frictionless UX to get everyone onboard.
  • Avoid Dashlane if you already have a dedicated VPN - you're paying for a bundled feature you don't need.
  • Avoid browser-only solutions (Chrome Password Manager, Firefox Lockwise) for teams. No shared vaults, no admin controls, no audit trail. Fine for personal use; dangerous for business.

FAQ

Is a password manager really necessary if we use SSO?

Yes. SSO covers apps that support SAML/OIDC, but most teams have dozens of logins that don't support SSO - legacy tools, shared social media accounts, Wi-Fi passwords, API keys, and personal accounts used for work. A password manager handles everything SSO doesn't.

What if someone forgets their master password?

1Password's admin recovery (Business tier) lets an admin initiate account recovery without knowing the employee's password. NordPass Business and Bitwarden Enterprise have admin-assisted recovery too. On lower tiers, recovery depends on the Emergency Kit (1Password) or recovery codes. Set up recovery options during onboarding, not after a lockout.

Should I use Bitwarden's self-hosting or their cloud?

Use the cloud unless you have a specific requirement (data sovereignty, air gap, compliance). Self-hosting means you're responsible for uptime, backups, security patches, and SSL certificates. Most teams under 100 people should use the managed cloud and spend their time on things that aren't server maintenance.

Can I share passwords with clients or contractors?

Yes. 1Password supports guest accounts with automatic expiry. NordPass Business and Bitwarden have organization-level sharing with configurable access. All three let you share specific vaults or items without exposing your full password database. Revoke access instantly when the engagement ends.

Verdict

Best value for teams: NordPass Business - XChaCha20 encryption, dark web monitoring, shared vaults with role control, and the lowest per-seat cost among premium options at $4.99/user/month. The ideal choice for teams already in the Nord ecosystem or those wanting strong security without the 1Password price tag.

Best UX and security: 1Password - best balance of security, usability, and team features for 2026. Watchtower, Secret Key architecture, and the most polished UX drive team adoption. Start with Teams Starter ($19.95/mo for up to 10) and move to Business when you need SSO.

Best budget option: Bitwarden - open source, self-hostable, and the cheapest per-seat cost. The right pick for budget-conscious teams and organizations that need full control over where their data lives.
